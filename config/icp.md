# Lysoveta ICP — Europe

## About Lysoveta
Lysoveta is a premium, clinically documented lysophosphatidylcholine (LPC)-enriched krill oil ingredient from Aker BioMarine, designed for brain health and cognitive function. The ingredient is sold B2B to European companies that manufacture finished products for consumers.

---

## Label Classification

### early_mover
Primary target — 2026 conversion goal

**Who they are:**
- Smaller to mid-size innovative nutraceutical or supplement brands driven by science storytelling and differentiation
- Pharma Rx companies with portfolios in cognition, metabolism, cardiovascular, geriatrics, or women's health, seeking clinically documented supplement products that physicians are confident to recommend

**Signals to look for:**
- Premium or prestige pricing — minimum ~$60/€55 per product unit is a strong positive signal
- Positioning around longevity, brain regeneration, neuroprotection, or preventive brain health (not just general wellness)
- Science-driven self-presentation: "clinical evidence", "science storytelling", "differentiation", "innovation"
- Sells primarily via DTC e-commerce, premium specialty retail, or online platforms
- For Pharma Rx companies: pharmacy distribution through physician prescription, HCP detailing, clinical conferences. In Europe, Pharma Rx companies with Rx portfolios in cognition, geriatrics, or neurology are especially strategic due to the market's HCP-dependency and pharmacy-channel conservatism.
- Target consumers: 30+, high income, biohackers, high performers, longevity enthusiasts, health-optimized consumers, preventive health adopters, executive health seekers, patients guided by healthcare professionals (45+)
- Strong positive signal if the brand explicitly targets: APOE4 carriers, individuals with genetic risk of cognitive decline, or family history of neurodegeneration — these are high-intent, science-oriented consumers who align directly with Lysoveta's clinical positioning
- Peak performance and cognitive fatigue are relevant positioning angles — treat them as early mover signals if combined with science-driven self-presentation

**Company stage (positive signal, not negative):**
- Single-product companies and early-stage brands are an intentional part of the target group, not noise
- Founder-led or celebrity-fronted brands (e.g. talent-backed brands) are a positive signal, not a minus
- A visible founder as the face of the brand is a marker of the right brand archetype — treat it as confirmation, not a red flag
- Low revenue history or short track record must NOT trigger exclusion
- Small company size is NOT an exclusion criterion

**Competitor / adjacent ingredient signal (positive):**
Companies that already have products containing the following ingredients are strong candidates for adding Lysoveta to their portfolio: Citicoline (CDP-choline), Phosphatidylserine, Alpha-GPC, Nicotinamide Riboside (NR), NMN, PQQ, Urolithin A, Spermidine. If confirmed, this should be noted explicitly and treated as a scoring bonus.

---

### follower
Secondary target — longer term (2027–2028)

**Who they are:**
- Large global or regional CHC companies (Haleon, Bayer, P&G, Reckitt-type)
- Established brands with long heritage and broad consumer base

**Signals to look for:**
- Competitive or mid-range pricing aimed at scale and volume
- Mainstream brain health positioning: cognitive performance, stress resilience, mood balance
- Science narrative present but secondary to heritage, brand recognition, or broad appeal
- Distributed via mass market, grocery chains, large pharmacy networks, specialty stores
- Target consumers: broad wellness consumers, working professionals, women

---

### enabler
Supporting role — not a primary customer, but important for distribution and formulation

**Who they are:**
- In-country distributors enabling market access for supplement ingredients and products
- CMOs (Contract Manufacturers) with softgel expertise producing for brand owners

**Signals to look for:**
- Sells or distributes other brands' products, not their own consumer products
- Offers production or distribution services to the supplement industry
- Does not have their own consumer-facing brand

## Inclusion Criteria

A company must meet ALL of the following to be included as early_mover or follower:

**1. Product portfolio angle**
The company's portfolio must include at least one of the following:
- Brain health, cognitive performance, memory, or neuroprotection
- Longevity, healthy aging, NAD+/NR, or anti-aging
- Omega-3, krill oil, or marine lipids
- Women's hormonal health or menopause support
- Preventive health or precision nutrition with a science narrative
- Pharma Rx in cognition, geriatrics, neurology, or cardiovascular

**2. Own-branded products**
The company must develop and sell its own branded consumer or professional products. Companies whose primary business is reselling or distributing other brands' products (e.g. large pharmacy retailers, health food chains) should be classified as enabler — not early_mover or follower.

**3. Some science narrative**
The company must have at least a basic science or health-claims narrative. Purely price- and convenience-driven brands with no science positioning are excluded.

**4. Market reach**
The company must sell in at least one of: EU, UK, US, or APAC. If market reach is unconfirmed (not found in sources, but not actively blocked), do NOT exclude — include the company and flag it in the description: "EU/UK distribution unconfirmed — verify before outreach." Only exclude if distribution is explicitly and actively limited to a non-target market (e.g. "ships to US only, no international orders").

**5. Company size and track record**
Company size, revenue history, number of products, and track record length are NEVER grounds for exclusion.
